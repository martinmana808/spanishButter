Explanation of the Tenses (Quick Guide)
  • Present → what happens now or usually.
    Ex: I speak with my family.

  • Preterite (simple past) → a completed action in the past, one specific moment.
    Ex: Yesterday I spoke with my aunt.

  • Imperfect (past) → a past action that was repeated, habitual, or "in progress."
    Ex: When I was a child, I used to speak a lot with my grandma.

  • Conditional → something hypothetical, what "would" happen.
    Ex: I would speak if I had time.

  • Infinitive → base form of the verb, unconjugated.
    Ex: to speak = hablar.

  • Gerund → the "-ing" form, ongoing action. Always used with ESTAR.
    Ex: Estoy hablando = I am speaking.

  • Participle → used in compound tenses with HABER or as an adjective.
    Ex: He hablado = I have spoken.

  • Simple Future (Periphrastic) → "going to..." in English. Structure: ir (present) + a + infinitive.
    Ex: Mañana voy a comprar un auto = Tomorrow I'm going to buy a car.
